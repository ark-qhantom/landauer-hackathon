# revenue-ops-lead-to-revenue

High-volume, repeatable lead research, qualification, personalized outreach, and conversion tracking service for small service businesses. Delivers qualified leads and booked calls. Uses real Stripe skills for client billing (per-lead or subscription). Focuses on measurable, redundant tasks that generate reliable revenue. Compounds on what actually drives conversions.

## When to Use
- Client service business needs steady pipeline of qualified leads/booked calls but lacks sales team bandwidth or wants scalable, governed ops.
- Tasks are redundant: research prospects, score fit, draft outreach, track responses/conversions, report ROI.
- Revenue model: Charge per qualified lead or monthly sub. Agent sets up billing via Hermes Stripe skills.
- Emphasis on redundancy and scale: High volume, repeatable, with clear metrics (conversion rate, cost per lead, revenue generated).

## Workflow (follow exactly, compound learnings)
1. Research vertical, ICP, and lead sources using web_search/browser.
2. Build and enrich prospect lists (tools for data).
3. Qualify leads (fit score, budget signals, intent) – use model + research.
4. Draft personalized outreach (email/DM sequences).
5. Setup client billing: Use Stripe skills to create products/prices/checkouts or subscriptions for the service. Human approves activation.
6. Execute outreach (via tools or simulate), track opens/replies/conversions.
7. Quality review: Human approves top leads/outreach before live or billing.
8. Report: Deliver leads, booked calls, ROI metrics.
9. Compound: Update this skill with what drove actual conversions (e.g. "personalized pain-point hooks in outreach increased reply rate 3x for contractors").
10. Allocate & spawn: Human allocates revenue; use portion to seed volume agents for scale.

## Anti-Slop / Quality
- All outputs (lists, scores, copy) must be specific, researched, personalized.
- Use references from previous successful campaigns in compounding.
- Quality gate before any client-facing or billing action.
- Focus on redundant but high-ROI tasks: volume qualification + tracking beats one-off creative.
